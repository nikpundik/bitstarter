Background

Global Postural Re-education (RPG) is a method of posture correction introduced by Philippe E. Souchard in 1980. It is an active and progressive method which is indicated to patients presenting postural ailments or for any musculoskeletal disorder.

The RPG treatments are based on the individuality of each person and they require accurate analysis and observation of anatomy and physiology. The plan of treatment identified by the specialist starts with the presentation of symptons. Visual observation and posture grid mirror are the common tools used by specialists to support posture correction.
Althought the grid mirror can give a fast feedback, it presents two major drawbacks:

  • the mirror is set behind\aside the patient
  • the mirror reflects the image horizontally

The patient is therefore forced to rotate his head to look at the mirrors, losing the correct spinal aligment.
The Postural Recognition System aims to overcome these drawbacks giving an immediate feedback both to the patient and to the specialist, projecting patient's back interest points and axis to a screen. The patient can easily look at the screen in front of him without turning his head.

The system

The Postural Recognition System (PRS) is a motion sensing system, capable of projecting patient's back interest points on screen. Working in real-time, the system is able to show an instant feedback of possible spinal misalignment, both to the specialist and to the patient.
The PRS works with a common camera and is able to recognize and isolate colored markers painted on the patient's back. Common body painting colors work well and are really easy to remove.
The system can track two different colors, therefore horizontal and vertical axis can be drawn over the points.
The PRS is meant to be an affordable solution for physiotherapists or kinesiologists who want to speed up postural reeducation.

Other features

Other features of the system are:

  • take a snapshot of patient's back
  • take a snapshot of patient's nodes and axes
  • toggle axis on and off
  • record a PRS session
  • upload a PRS session in the PRS desktop, a web portal collecting all the data that can also be shared anonymously between specialists
  • see a recorded PRS session with the PRS html5\jQuery viewer
  • compare recorded PRS sessions during the treatment

FAQ

What kind of camera is needed?
A medium level web camera is sufficient.
What has been done until now?
We have a working prototype that has already been tested by some kinesiologists. The interface is still minimal, but the results are already interesting.
Is the video representing the PRS?
That video was shot at a very early stage, the axis are not shown and the color tracking is not very accurate. We will provide a new video soon.
